My Favorite Hobby is Fishing!

Fishing is both a relaxing pastime and an exciting sport enjoyed worldwide. Whether casting lines into the vast ocean for saltwater species like tuna and redfish or targeting largemouth bass in freshwater lakes, fishing offers a unique connection to nature. Saltwater fishing provides thrilling battles with powerful fish, while bass fishing challenges anglers with strategy and technique. No matter the setting, fishing is a rewarding experience that combines skill, patience, and the thrill of the catch.

Best Time to Fish

In my opinion, the best time to go fishing is right before sunset because fish are most active during this time. As the sun sets, the water cools, and many species move closer to shore or the surface to feed. In saltwater inlets, an outgoing tide just before sunset creates strong currents that carry baitfish out to sea, attracting predatory fish like redfish and snook. In freshwater, bass tend to be more aggressive in the evening, making it the perfect time to cast a line and enjoy the peaceful end of the day.

Best places to go Fishing

Fishing jetties and inlets are some of the best spots for saltwater fishing because they attract a variety of fish that thrive in strong currents and structure. The rocks and pilings create hiding spots for baitfish, drawing in predators like snook, redfish, and flounder. The changing tides also play a big role, making certain times of the day especially productive. For freshwater fishing, private ponds are unbeatable for bass fishing. With less pressure from other anglers, bass in these waters tend to be more aggressive and willing to bite. These secluded spots offer a peaceful fishing experience and often produce bigger, healthier fish compared to public lakes.

  • Top Saltwater Locations: Inlets, Jetties, Piers, Marinas
  • Top Freshwater Locations: Any private pond that you are not allowed to fish in.
  • Best Conditions: Low tide for inlets, calm weather for ponds, sound-side marinas when windy

How to Get Started

Getting started with fishing at the beach or a pond is simple and doesnâ€™t require expensive gear. For beach fishing, a medium-heavy spinning rod, a sand spike, and a basic rig with shrimp or cut bait are great for targeting species like whiting, redfish, and pompano. Pay attention to tides and look for sandbars or deeper troughs where fish gather. For pond fishing, a light or medium spinning setup with soft plastics, worms, or topwater lures is perfect for catching bass and panfish. Focus on casting near structures like lily pads or fallen logs where fish like to hide. With a little patience and practice, anyone can enjoy a great day of fishing.
